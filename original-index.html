Personal Story

Born in the Republic of Congo where I earned a degree in Physics, I have been a British resident for 23 years now.

After dropping out of BSc Computing at Leeds University, School of Computing, due to financial hurdles, I was able to earn my first computing qualification a few years later after successful completion of an intensive Microsoft/City & Guild partnership program in PC Engeneering and Network Technology.

Despite my computing obsession, I had an ambition for showbusiness, which was my main passion until recent years. Up until recently, I worked as a self employed most of the time, balancing between my ambition in show business and IT. In 2018, I decided to take on IT in full. And there begun a never-ending journey of self development. Ever since, I have continuously taken on further education and business development courses in computing.

Intially, I began a journey with CISCO to follow on my networking background; however, I quickly gravitated to cloud computing as I anticipated the future of computing is driven towards cloud computing. So I went on studying Azure, setting up as a self employed to help me complete my qualifications.

The journey with Azure was particularly awkward for me as, when I first got into it, I had no programming experience. Essentially, I had to learn programming on the go to complete some of the tasks required for my journey with Azure. Part of the courses that I took included courses from Harvard University YouTube channel and more.

Within 5 years I was able to evolve from fundamentals to expert. After I certifed as a Microsoft Azure Solutions Architect, I took on further courses in data engineering, security, etc. It became very clear to me that I needed to gain recognition and experience with computer science if I had to succeed as a computer expert.

At this point, I decided to do some research; and this is how I discovered CS50. I have worked tireless on each and every assignment to help me complete the program as soon as possible, as effectively as I can, and here we are.

So far what I have come to learn at Harvard University has overwhelmingly surpassed my expectation, by far, essentially giving me not just all the code experience I sought, but also the intellectual challenges I needed at this stage of my career; and most importantly, with CS50 I have learned to think not just like a programmer, but like a computer scientist.

Introduction

I took on CS50 in part because of my passion for learning, because of my passion for IT, but also because, as a computer expert coming from a networking, PC engineering, database, and cloud background, I determined that instead of learning a program here and there as and when needed as I did previously, it would benefit to understand the science of computing. I am destined to work side by side with developers. Areas of expertise detailed below.

As part of CS50 at Harvard University, over the course of 6 months I successfully solved 9 labs, 9 problems sets, and developed a final project, which ultimately earned me a completion certificate; I scored maximum marks on all, as evidenced here: My SC50 Score. The on-campus version of CS50 is Harvard's largest program. To complete this program, in parrallel, I took additional courses namely CS50W, CS50 Python, advanced Git/GitHub, and more. Programs learned at Harvard include C, Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, SQL, Flask & more. This page is adaptation of a website I designed as part of CS50 assignment; a work I've produced entirely.
